## What the product actually does

The Duct Lead Qualifier answers a business's Facebook Messenger inbox
automatically, around the clock, as a named company representative. When a
customer messages the page, it:

- Replies in seconds, day or night, instead of the lead going cold.
- Runs a real qualification flow: what service, what area, how many units,
  what price, then name, phone, and address. One question at a time. It never
  re-asks something the customer already answered.
- **Checks the service area before quoting**, so the business never commits to
  a job outside its coverage. Out-of-area customers get a polite decline, and
  the owner gets a quiet note that demand exists there.
- **Quotes only the prices the business configured.** It never invents a
  number. Discount requests get escalated to a human instead of guessed at.
- **Books a genuinely open slot**, accounting for business hours, existing
  jobs, job duration, travel buffer, and how many crews can work at once. No
  same-day, no closed days, no double-booking.
- Recognizes returning customers by name and skips what it already knows.
- Hands off to the owner when it hits something it should not answer, and goes
  silent the moment the owner takes over.
- Emails the owner the finished lead: name, phone, address, service, price,
  appointment time.
- Keeps every lead and full transcript in a dashboard with a calendar view.

The owner spends no time until there is a confirmed job on the calendar.

## How to close

One simple, low-pressure question. Something they can answer with a single
line, or ignore without feeling pursued.

Good: "Want one of the five spots?" / "Worth a look?" / "Should I send over
what setup involves?"

Bad: "Are you available Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am?" (presumptuous),
"Don't miss out on this limited opportunity!" (manufactured urgency), "Let me
know if you'd like to hop on a quick 15-minute discovery call to explore
synergies" (nobody talks like this).
